# Mission GroundWork Readiness Diagnostic — Pilot Packet v0.1

## Purpose

Govern the first real or proxy test of the Readiness Diagnostic. The pilot must reveal whether the diagnostic is clear, useful, proportionate, and capable of changing a real decision.

## Pilot boundary

Use one real upcoming hire, program launch, leadership transition, partnership, or operational change. A proxy is permitted only when the scenario includes concrete evidence and a real decision.

## Acceptance test

The alpha passes only when:

1. Completion takes 35 minutes or less for a respondent familiar with the organization.
2. At least 85% of statements are understandable without facilitator explanation.
3. The respondent identifies three prioritized repairs.
4. The final decision is explicit: proceed, proceed with repairs, narrow, delay, or replace with groundwork.
5. Missing evidence is identifiable.
6. The result changes or materially strengthens at least one decision, assumption, or ownership assignment.

## Revision triggers

Revise when:

- Two or more respondents interpret a statement differently.
- A dimension cannot be supported by observable evidence.
- The scoring band conflicts sharply with documented risk.
- The tool recommends delay without identifying a minimum viable repair.
- Completion exceeds 45 minutes.
- Respondents confuse organizational conditions with individual performance.

## Evidence integrity

A positive reaction is not sufficient. Each pilot must produce timing, ambiguity notes, missing-evidence notes, a decision outcome, and a repair sequence.
