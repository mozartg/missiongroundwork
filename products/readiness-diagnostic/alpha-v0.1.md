# Mission GroundWork Readiness Diagnostic

**Alpha v0.1**

Helping your team start on solid ground.

## Purpose

This diagnostic helps a nonprofit determine whether the conditions around a new hire, new program, major initiative, or operational transition are strong enough for the work to begin responsibly.

It is not a personality test and it does not grade the person entering the role. It examines the ground the organization is asking them to stand on.

## Rating scale

- **0** — Not present
- **1** — Present informally or inconsistently
- **2** — Mostly present but incomplete
- **3** — Clear, current, owned, and usable

Score only what can be located and used now.

## Eight readiness dimensions

1. Mission and outcome clarity
2. Authority and decision rights
3. Ownership and handoffs
4. Systems and access
5. Information and institutional knowledge
6. Capacity and workload
7. Measurement and feedback
8. Trust, communication, and working conditions

Each section contains five scored statements, an evidence field, and one repair action.

## Readiness bands

- **96–120 — Solid Ground**
- **72–95 — Workable Ground With Repairs**
- **48–71 — Unstable Ground**
- **0–47 — Do Not Treat This as an Onboarding Problem**

## Priority test

For every section scoring below 9:

1. What could fail because this ground is missing?
2. Who is affected?
3. What is the smallest repair that materially changes the risk?
4. Who owns that repair?
5. What evidence will show it is complete?

## 90-day structure

- **Days 1–30: Stabilize** — choose no more than three conditions that prevent safe or useful work.
- **Days 31–60: Connect** — repair ownership, handoffs, access, and decision interfaces.
- **Days 61–90: Strengthen** — measure what changed and decide which remaining weaknesses require a larger engagement.

## Final readiness decision

- Proceed normally
- Proceed with named repairs
- Narrow the launch
- Delay until minimum ground exists
- Replace the initiative with a groundwork engagement

## Evidence boundaries

This alpha has not been externally validated. Its scoring bands are design hypotheses, not scientifically validated thresholds.

A pilot must test:

- completion time
- unclear or overlapping questions
- scoring disagreement
- whether the diagnostic changes a real decision
- whether it identifies a useful repair
- whether a guided or paid version is warranted
